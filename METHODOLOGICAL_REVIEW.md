# SPECTER Methodological Review: Honest Assessment

## Executive Summary

**Original Claim**: 8.32x elevation in SF Bay UFO reports during seismically active periods, suggesting piezoelectric phenomena.

**After Rigorous Review**: The evidence is **substantially weaker** than originally presented. Several criticisms are valid.

---

## Criticism-by-Criticism Assessment

### 1. M≥1.0 Threshold Too Low
**VERDICT: VALID**

| Threshold | Ratio | P-value | Signal |
|-----------|-------|---------|--------|
| M≥1.0 | 8.32x | <0.0001 | Strong |
| M≥4.0 | **0.62x** | 0.0076 | **INVERTED** |

The signal **disappears** with rare earthquakes. At M≥4.0, we see FEWER reports than expected before earthquakes. This is a critical failure of the precursor hypothesis.

**Implication**: The M≥1.0 threshold created near-continuous "active" windows in the Bay Area, artificially inflating the correlation.

---

### 2. Population Density Confound
**VERDICT: PARTIALLY VALID (but complicated)**

| Region | Population | Reports | Per 100K |
|--------|------------|---------|----------|
| SF Bay Area | 2.3M | 490 | 21.3 |
| Portland Metro | 650K | 398 | **61.2** |

**Surprise finding**: Portland has **2.9x MORE** UFO reports per capita than SF Bay.

This actually *helps* the SPECTER hypothesis in one way: if Portland has more observers but lower seismic-UFO correlation, that suggests SF's correlation isn't purely about observation opportunity.

However, it also shows the "hotspot" framing was misleading—SF isn't special for UFO reporting, just for seismic activity.

---

### 3. Multiple Testing
**VERDICT: VALID CONCERN**

| Correction | Tests Surviving | Out of |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| None | 7 | 10 |
| Bonferroni | 5 | 10 |
| FDR | 7 | 10 |

**Tests that survive Bonferroni:**
- SF seismic correlation (p<0.0001)
- Magnetic-UFO correlation (p<0.0001)
- Precursor window M≥1.0 (p=0.001)
- Shape-geology association (p=0.002)
- Orb clustering (p=0.003)

**Tests that fail:**
- M≥4.0 precursor (p=0.0076) — **critical test fails**
- Loma Prieta spike (p=0.01)

---

### 4. Portland Skepticism Should Apply to SF
**VERDICT: MIXED**

| Factor | SF Bay | Portland |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Clear sky days/year | 260 | 140 |
| Per-capita reports | 21.3 | 61.2 |
| Seismic-UFO ratio | 8.32 | 3.44 |

SF has **better** observation conditions but **fewer** per-capita reports, yet shows **higher** seismic correlation. This pattern is consistent with a real seismic-UFO link rather than pure observer bias.

However, the M≥4.0 failure undermines this argument significantly.

---

### 5. Temporal Holdout Test
**VERDICT: INCONCLUSIVE**

The post-2015 analysis could not be completed due to data limitations (NUFORC dataset may not include recent years in the version used).

---

## What Survives Scrutiny

### Strong Evidence
1. **Magnetic anomaly correlation** (rho=-0.497, survives Bonferroni)
   - Low magnetic signature correlates with UFO report clusters
   - This is the most robust finding

2. **SF vs Portland observation-corrected comparison**
   - Portland's higher per-capita reporting + lower seismic correlation suggests SF's pattern isn't pure bias

### Weak/Failed Evidence
1. **Precursor hypothesis** — FAILS at M≥4.0 threshold
2. **8.32x elevation claim** — Artifact of low earthquake threshold
3. **Loma Prieta "smoking gun"** — Does not survive multiple testing correction

---

## Revised Conclusions

### Original SPECTER Claims vs Reality

| Claim | Original | After Review |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| SF elevation ratio | 8.32x | **0.62x** (M≥4.0) |
| P-value | <0.0001 | 0.0076 (doesn't survive Bonferroni) |
| Effect size | "Strong" | **Weak to none** |
| Mechanism | Piezoelectric precursors | **Unsubstantiated** |

### What We Can Actually Say

1. **There IS a correlation** between low-magnetic geology and UFO report clustering. This survives all corrections.

2. **There is NOT strong evidence** for earthquake precursor lights. The signal disappears with rare (M≥4.0) earthquakes.

3. **The Loma Prieta observation** is suggestive but could be coincidence (doesn't survive multiple testing).

4. **Population bias** doesn't fully explain the patterns, but the original analysis overstated the effect.

---

## Methodological Lessons

1. **Always use stringent thresholds** for rare event analysis
2. **Report all p-values with multiple testing corrections**
3. **Per-capita normalization** is essential for geographic comparisons
4. **Holdout validation** should be standard practice

---

## Honest Bottom Line

The SPECTER analysis found something real in the magnetic-UFO correlation. However, the earthquake precursor hypothesis—the headline finding—does not survive rigorous scrutiny. The 8.32x ratio was an artifact of methodology, not evidence of piezoelectric phenomena.

**Grade: C-**
- Creative hypothesis: A
- Data collection: A
- Statistical rigor: D
- Replicability: F (can't replicate with M≥4.0)

The magnetic anomaly finding deserves follow-up research. The precursor claim should be retracted or heavily caveated.
